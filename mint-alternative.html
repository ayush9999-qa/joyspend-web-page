What Happened to Mint?

Mint was one of the most popular budgeting apps in the world, with over 20 million users tracking their spending for free. In January 2024, its parent company Intuit shut it down permanently and redirected users to Credit Karma — a credit score tool that does not replace Mint's core expense tracking features.

Millions of users were left searching for a replacement. If you're one of them, JoySpend was built for exactly this moment — a clean, private, genuinely free alternative with no corporate agenda and no data selling.
